What is Being Proposed?

The City Council is being asked to formally adopt a Resolution to Vacate (Resolution No. 25-1401340) for a portion of McKinley Place located southerly of 87th Street in Council District 9. This action follows up on a previous City Council decision from September 22, 2020, and implements that decision through an accelerated review process.

Why?

The vacation of this street portion was previously approved by the City Council on September 22, 2020. No protests were filed against the vacation at that time, and all conditions imposed by the Council have been fulfilled. The current action is simply finalizing and formalizing what was already approved five years ago, as instructed by the City Council. The resolution has been reviewed and approved by the City Attorney for form and legality.

Key Details

Location: Portion of McKinley Place south of 87th Street, Council District 9 Case Number: VAC – E1401340 Original Approval: September 22, 2020 Environmental Status: The project is exempt from California Environmental Quality Act requirements under categorical exemptions (Class 5(3)) Charter Compliance: The action complies with Section 556 of the Los Angeles City Charter

Impact

Adopting this resolution formally removes this street portion from public right-of-way. This allows the affected property owners or developers to use the vacated street area for private purposes. The lack of protests when originally proposed suggests minimal community opposition.

What is Being Proposed?

The Los Angeles City Council is being asked to approve the vacation (removal from public ownership) of a 32.5-foot by 135-foot portion of McKinley Place, located southerly of 87th Street. This means the City would give up its ownership and maintenance responsibilities for this small section of street, and it would likely become private property or be incorporated into an adjacent property.

Why?

The City identifies several benefits to this vacation. Most importantly, it would eliminate the City's ongoing obligation to maintain this portion of street and remove the City's potential liability from owning the easement. The vacation is determined to be unnecessary for current or future public use and is not needed for non-motorized transportation (like bike paths). The petitioner (the property owner requesting the vacation) has already paid a $14,980 deposit to cover investigation costs.

Key Details

Location: Portion of McKinley Place south of 87th Street (Lot 12 of Tract 473) Committee Vote: Unanimously approved (5-0) on August 19, 2020 CEQA Status: Exempt from environmental review Petitioner Costs: Already paid $14,980; may owe additional fees for deficit costs

Impact

This primarily affects the property owner adjacent to the street section, who would gain access to this land. Los Angeles residents would see a minor reduction in publicly maintained street. The City saves on maintenance costs and liability exposure. The document notes no community impact statement was submitted, suggesting minimal public concern was raised.

What is Being Proposed?

North East Trees, represented by Project Manager George Baliteria, is applying to vacate a public right of way located at McKinley Place @ 87th Street in Los Angeles (Council District 9). The proposed vacation area is approximately 4,387.3 square feet and is situated between McKinley Avenue & McKinley Place and 87th Street and 87th Place.

Why?

The vacation is being requested to enable the development of a neighborhood park on the currently vacant and asphalt-paved parcel. The project will convert an underutilized public right of way into a community green space, approximately half an acre in size. The vacation is being pursued in conjunction with a B-Permit (BR 003792).

Key Details

Project Manager/Petitioner: George Baliteria, North East Trees, 570 West Avenue 26, Suite 200, Los Angeles, CA 90065 Property Owner: City of Los Angeles, with signatory Shelley Luce (President & CEO) Size: Approximately 4,387.3 square feet Location: Central Engineering District, Council District 9, District Map Nos. 096 A 207 & 097 SA 207 Application Date: March 29, 2018

Impact

This project will benefit the local community by transforming an underutilized asphalt-covered area into accessible green space. The vacation is small enough (under 10,000 sq. ft.) to potentially qualify for categorical exemption from California Environmental Quality Act review, though residents should note that city agencies like LADOT may require additional fees for referral and investigation.

What is Being Proposed?

The City is being asked to approve the vacation (removal from public use) of a 32.5-foot by 135-foot portion of McKinley Place south of 87th Street in Council District 9. Once vacated, this street segment would be transferred to the adjacent private property owner, North East Trees (representing Heal the Bay), to create a neighborhood park instead of maintaining it as a public street.

Why?

The street segment is underutilized and not essential for public circulation. The petitioner plans to develop the vacated area into a community park featuring shade trees, gardens, fitness stations, a walking track, and outdoor classrooms to serve students at Wisdom Academy for Young Scientists (WAYS) and the surrounding South Los Angeles community. The city would benefit by eliminating ongoing maintenance costs and potential liability associated with owning this street easement.

Key Details

Location: Portion of McKinley Place south of 87th Street, identified as the northerly portion of Lot 12 of Tract 473 Applicant: George Ballteria on behalf of North East Trees Timeline: Initial approval was February 6, 2019; this corrected report is dated August 13, 2020 Conditions: The petitioner must dedicate 3 additional feet to McKinley Avenue, construct various street improvements (sidewalks, curbs, gutters, and curb ramps), plant street trees, and install lighting Fiscal Impact: The petitioner has already paid a $14,980 deposit; any additional costs will be billed to them

Impact

Who: Residents and students in the underserved Southeast Los Angeles area gain a new recreational facility; the City reduces maintenance obligations. Support: The Fire Department, City Planning, and Department of Transportation all approved the project with conditions. Process: The vacation requires a public hearing and final City Council vote after committee approval.

What is Being Proposed?

The City Engineer is recommending that the Los Angeles City Council vacate (remove from public ownership) a 32.5-foot by 135-foot section of McKinley Place, located south of 87th Street in Council District 9. The vacated street would be transferred to North East Trees, representing Heal the Bay, which is the current lessee of the adjoining property.

Why?

The petitioner wants to convert the underutilized street into a neighborhood park that will serve Wisdom Academy for Young Scientists (WAYS) students and the surrounding community. The proposed improvements include shade trees, a garden, fitness stations, a walking track, and outdoor classrooms. The City would benefit by being relieved of ongoing street maintenance obligations and potential liability associated with owning the street easement.

Key Details

The application was submitted on March 29, 2018, and the City Council initiated vacation proceedings in February 2019. The petitioner has already paid $14,980 in investigation fees. The vacation was found to be consistent with the City's General Plan and the Southeast Los Angeles Community Plan's goals for open space and recreation. No objections were received from the public, and city departments—including the Fire Department, Planning Department, and Department of Transportation—approved the request with conditions.

Impact

The vacation will require the petitioner to dedicate additional street width to McKinley Avenue and construct significant improvements to adjacent streets (McKinley Place, 87th Street, and McKinley Avenue), including new sidewalks, curbs, gutters, and curb ramps. A 2-year timeline has been set for the petitioner to complete all conditions. Once vacated, the street area becomes private property for the community park project, benefiting local residents while removing city maintenance responsibilities.

What is Being Proposed?

The City of Los Angeles is initiating a vacation proceeding to vacate (permanently close) a portion of McKinley Place located south of 87th Street. This is the first step in a formal legal process that would allow the currently paved, vacant street to be converted into a neighborhood park on approximately half an acre of land.

Why?

The petitioner, North East Trees (representing Heal The Bay), submitted this request to transform underutilized public right-of-way into green space for community benefit. The street vacation process is governed by California Streets and Highways Code and requires investigation of feasibility before proceeding to a public hearing and City Council vote.

Key Details

Filing Date: March 29, 2018 Case Number: VAC E1401340 Location: McKinley Place south of 87th Street (Council District 9) Petitioner Fee: $14,980.00 paid for investigation costs Proposed Size: Approximately ½ acre Current Condition: Vacant and paved with asphalt

Impact & Next Steps

This report does not approve the vacation—it merely initiates the investigation process. The Bureau of Engineering will study feasibility, present findings to the Public Works and Gang Reduction Committee, and eventually schedule a public hearing before the City Council makes a final decision. Residents and stakeholders will have opportunity to comment during that public hearing phase.
