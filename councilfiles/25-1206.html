What is Being Proposed

The Trade, Travel and Tourism Committee is recommending City Council approval of a Third Amendment to the construction contract with Swinerton Builders for offsite roadway improvements at Los Angeles International Airport (LAX). The amendment would reallocate $5 million from the project's contingency fund to the main contract, increase the total contract amount from $234 million to $239 million, and extend the project completion deadline by 13 months to February 26, 2027.

Why This is Needed

The amendment addresses funding and scheduling needs for the Landside Access Modernization Program's Intermodal Transportation Facility (ITF) Auxiliary Curbs Project. The $5 million reallocation and 13-month extension appear necessary to complete the offsite roadway work as currently planned.

Key Details

Contract: DA-5605 with Swinerton Builders Funding reallocation: $5 million from project contingency to contract authority New contract amount: $239,095,953 (previously $234,095,953) Overall project budget: Remains unchanged at $289,737,127 New completion date: February 26, 2027 Committee vote: Unanimous approval (Park, McOsker, Rodriguez all voted YES) Date: October 28, 2025 committee meeting

Impact

This affects LAX operations and airport access improvements. The financial impact on the City is neutral—no new funding is required, as the money comes from existing project reserves. The work will continue modernizing ground transportation access to the airport.

What is Being Proposed?

The Board of Airport Commissioners is requesting City Council approval for a Third Amendment to an existing contract with Swinerton Builders. The amendment would add $5,000,000 to the project allowance and extend the contract term by 13 months to complete offsite work related to the Landside Access Modernization Program at Los Angeles International Airport (LAX).

Why?

The document does not explicitly state the reasons for the amendment. However, the extension of the contract timeline and increased funding suggest that additional work or unforeseen circumstances require more time and resources to complete the Intermodal Transportation Facility Auxiliary Curbs Project—part of LAX's broader modernization effort to improve ground transportation access.

Key Details

Contractor: Swinerton Builders Contract Number: DA-5605 Additional Funding: Up to $5,000,000 Contract Extension: 13 months Project: Landside Access Modernization Program Intermodal Transportation Facility Auxiliary Curbs Project at LAX Approved by: Board of Airport Commissioners on August 21, 2025 (Resolution 28221) Impact to General Fund: None

Impact

This action affects LAX operations and transportation infrastructure improvements. There is no financial impact to the City's General Fund, suggesting the project is funded through airport revenues rather than general tax dollars. The extended timeline may affect the overall modernization program schedule at LAX.
